This is the final chapter in a remarkable mission of exploration and discovery...

A lone explorer, on a mission to reveal the grandeur of Saturn, its rings and moons.
After 20 years in space NASA's Cassini spacecraft was running out of fuel. And so, to protect moons of
Saturn that could have conditions suitable for life, a spectacular end was planned for this long-lived traveler from Earth.

For over a decade, Cassini has shared the wonders of Saturn and its family of icy and queer moons,
taking us to astounding worlds where methane rivers run to a methane sea -Titan

Where jets of ice and gas blast material into space from a liquid subsurface water ocean
that might harbor the ingredients for life - Enceladus

And Saturn -- a giant world ruled by raging storms and delicate harmonies of gravity.

In a whole new way, Cassini's Grand Finale is like a whole new mission.

It's a series of twenty-two orbits of saturn, intending to seek out the origins of the rings and the nature of the planet's interior itself.
